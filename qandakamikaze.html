Q&A

How did Kamikaze attacks impact the outcome of the war?

They did not have to much impact on the outcome of the war, but It did take out many Allied ships and killed many Allied soldiers.

Where Japanese soldiers forced to fly?

No, Kamikaze applicants were allowed to refuse to join, but when you accepted to sacrifice yourself for Japan and the Emperor Hirohito, you wouldn't be able to get out of it. Most Kamikaze pilots saw being able to die for their country as an honor as many Japanese soliers did which made it easier to get people to accept becoming one.

Why was the Kamikaze created?

The Kamikaze was made to be able to effectively deal heavy damage to Allied warships and were hoped to be able to reverse the tide of the war. Kamikazes were involved in many of the most bloodiest battles in the Pacific Ocean.
